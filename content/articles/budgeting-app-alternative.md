# budgeting app alternative

## Quick answer

If you’re tired of maintaining budgets, Pip offers a simpler way: one daily spending number that tells you what’s truly okay to spend today. Instead of splitting every expense into categories, Pip looks at your connected accounts, protects your savings, and subtracts near‑term bills and committed spending—so you get a single, honest number. It’s read‑only, doesn’t move money, and replaces the risky habit of checking a misleading bank balance.

## A realistic money example

Most bank balances look bigger than what’s actually spendable. Here’s how Pip turns a typical balance into a safe daily number:

| What you see                     | Amount    | How Pip helps                                        |
|----------------------------------|-----------|------------------------------------------------------|
| Bank balance                     | $2,500    | Appears high, but many obligations are already committed. |
| Near‑term bills (rent, utilities)| –$1,200   | Pip subtracts these before showing your number.     |
| Protected savings cushion        | –$500     | Pip sets aside your chosen cushion.                |
| Already‑committed card spending  | –$200     | Accounted for so the number stays honest.          |
| **Safe leftover**                | **$600**  | The actual pool of cash available through payday.    |
| Days until next income           | 7 days    | Pip divides the leftover across the remaining days. |
| **Spendable Cash Today**         | **≈$85/day** | Your daily spending signal—check it before you spend.|

Without Pip, that $2,500 balance might tempt someone to overspend by hundreds of dollars. With Pip, you see a number you can trust without building a budget.

## How to estimate it

The rough formula behind Spendable Cash Today is:

**Estimated spendable today = usable cash – near‑term bills – protected savings – already‑committed card/debit spending – other known obligations**

Pip computes this automatically from your connected, read‑only accounts. It identifies upcoming bills, sets aside the savings cushion you choose, and accounts for transactions that haven’t settled yet. The remaining amount is then divided by the days until your next income, producing a daily spending number you can rely on.

## Limits

A few situations can make the daily number less accurate:

- **Missing accounts** – If a checking, savings, or credit card isn’t connected, Pip can’t see the full picture.
- **Stale connections** – Some banks require re‑authorization; a stale connection can delay fresh transactions.
- **Delayed transactions** – Pending charges, tips, or refunds may not appear right away.
- **Unexpected bills** – One‑time expenses or annual subscriptions Pip hasn’t learned about won’t be subtracted.
- **Manual errors** – If your savings cushion is set too low or a recurring bill isn’t tagged, the number can drift.

That’s why Pip treats the number as a decision‑support signal, not a guarantee.

## How Pip handles it

Pip is built for people who don’t want to maintain a budget but still want a daily signal. Here’s what sets it apart:

- **Only one number** – No categories, no charts, no monthly rollovers. Just [Spendable Cash Today](/blog/what-is-spendable-cash-today).
- **Savings stay invisible** – You set a savings cushion inside the app, and Pip hides it from your daily number, so you don’t accidentally spend it.
- **Pacing built in** – Because the number covers the days until your next income and resets daily, it naturally encourages smaller, more thoughtful spending without a budgeting process.
- **Always read‑only** – Pip can only view transactions. It cannot move money, initiate payments, or change anything in your accounts.
- **Replaces the bank‑balance habit** – A raw bank balance often looks higher than what’s truly available. Pip’s number is deliberately smaller and more realistic, helping you avoid overspending. Read more about the problem [here](/blog/why-your-bank-balance-is-misleading).

## FAQ

### How is Pip different from a traditional budgeting app?

Traditional budgeting apps ask you to build and maintain categories, spending limits, and track every transaction. Pip skips all that. It shows a single daily number—Spendable Cash Today—based on your actual bills, savings, and commitments. If budgets have felt overwhelming before, one number is often simpler to follow.

### Does Pip replace my bank balance?

Yes, as a spending signal. Your bank balance may look high but hide upcoming bills and savings. Pip’s Spendable Cash Today filters out what’s already spoken for, giving you a number that’s safe to spend. For a deeper look, see [Why your bank balance is misleading](/blog/why-your-bank-balance-is-misleading).

### Is Pip read‑only and safe?

Pip is read‑only, does not move money, does not store bank usernames or passwords, and is not financial advice. Account connections use secure, bank‑level data aggregators. You can learn more on our [Security](/security) page.
