# Pip Check First Video Design

## Style Prompt

A warm, calm product-marketing loop for Pip, a soft sage-green daily money companion. The video should feel like the current Pip website: light canvas, quiet editorial spacing, clay-like product imagery, soft shadows, rounded phone/product moments, and restrained motion. The tone is useful and calm rather than dramatic fintech. The viewer should understand the promise in seconds: stop using bank balance as permission, check Spendable Cash Today, and ask Pip for the why.

## Colors

- Canvas: `#fcf9f8`
- Paper: `#fffdf8`
- Ink: `#1c1b1b`
- Muted copy: `#545a50`
- Sage accent: `#4e6545`
- Soft sage: `#8da682`
- Warm rule: `#e8ddd1`
- Warm wash: `#f4e8d7`

## Typography

- Display voice: `Georgia`, serif, used for the large money signal and hero statements.
- Utility/body voice: `Arial`, system sans-serif, used for labels, captions, and proof chips.
- Numbers use tabular numerals. Large text should carry the message; small labels should support the composition without becoming UI documentation.

## Motion

- Calm entrance motion: short offsets, soft scale, slow drift, and light-wash scene transitions.
- No jump cuts. Each new scene enters behind a warm wash, then its key elements animate in.
- Keep ambient motion subtle: phone/product drift, glow breathing, and small proof-chip movement only.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not present Pip as a bank, adviser, investment product, or money-movement app.
- Do not show real financial data, Plaid tokens, auth screens, dashboards, or personally identifying information.
- Do not use neon fintech colors, dark cyber gradients, hard chart dashboards, or aggressive sales language.
- Do not make Pip feel like a budgeting spreadsheet; the product promise is one calm daily signal plus Ask Pip context.
