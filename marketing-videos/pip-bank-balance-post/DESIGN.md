# Pip Bank Balance Post Design

## Style Prompt

A calm vertical product ad for Pip layered over warm clay-like source footage. The viewer should feel the contrast between a tempting bank balance and the more useful daily spending signal. The composition should preserve the soft sage, warm paper, and friendly Pip brand tone from the current marketing site while making the overlays crisp enough to read on a phone.

## Colors

- Canvas scrim: `rgba(252, 249, 248, 0.62)`
- Paper: `#fffdf8`
- Ink: `#1c1b1b`
- Muted copy: `#545a50`
- Sage accent: `#4e6545`
- Soft sage: `#8da682`
- Warm rule: `#e8ddd1`
- Warm wash: `#f4e8d7`

## Typography

- Display voice: `Georgia`, serif, used for the large money signals and final statement.
- Utility/body voice: `Arial`, system sans-serif, used for labels, badges, and ledger rows.
- Numbers use tabular numerals. Overlay text should stay large, direct, and readable at mobile social-video size.

## Motion

- Fast but gentle post-production pacing, because the supplied video is accelerated to match the shorter voiceover.
- Scene changes use warm wash transitions and transparent overlay crossovers so the source video stays continuous.
- Each overlay scene enters with varied short offsets, soft scale, and readable stagger timing.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not add the Pip logo before the final scene.
- Do not cover the shopper, phone, or Pip character with dense text.
- Do not present Pip as a bank, adviser, investment product, or money-movement app.
- Do not use dark fintech styling, neon gradients, chart dashboards, or aggressive sales language.
